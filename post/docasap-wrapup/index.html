DocASAP Wrap-up

Thu, Aug 4, 2016 work

The day we arrived at DocASAP, we were assigned our first task: build the desks that we’d be using for the rest of the summer. Assembling IKEA furniture was not how I expected to start my summer internship at the health tech company, but it offered a surprisingly fun introduction to the startup lifestyle.

Besides learning how to use a power drill (CEO Puneet Maheshwari was a great teacher), I have gained much from my experience at DocASAP. The first technical project I was assigned to was performance testing the front-end of the website. I became very familiar with the tool JMeter, a software that allows one to simulate and analyze the response times of 1000+ concurrent users accessing a website. We were able to isolate bottlenecks in the client-server flow in order to optimize performance.

Since then I have had the chance to work on many different projects:

  • UX/UI review for the patient booking flow

  • A database table migration, which was a nice introduction to PHP

  • SEO analysis for DocASAP’s Google White Labels

  • Researching database configurations (master/slave vs. clusters) in preparation for a possible database restructuring

  • Integrating the central database hub with external Electronic Practice Management (EPM) APIs

  • Refactoring the codebase of a 10000+ line backend project, which was a fascinating and challenging software engineering problem

  • Creating a fun company video! As of this time, the video is still in production, but once I get word that it’s finished, I’ll share a link to it

Leaving out the company video, this progression of projects was an increasingly challenging yet manageable journey through the roles a developer may hold at a tech company. Whereas before the operations of a professional software engineering team was somewhat of a mystery to me, I now feel confident that I could succeed in a professional environment such as this one. I’m especially thankful to have had the chance to work on these last couple of projects. I feel that I have made a significant contribution to my company’s core product, which is something that not all interns are able to say.

Wearing many hats is fairly typical for a developer at startup that is growing at the rate DocASAP is. Looking back, I am impressed by the company’s growth in just the three months that I’ve been here. Since arriving, I’ve witnessed many milestones for DocASAP, including reaching 1,000,000 appointments booked with one of our clients. The company is rapidly expanding, with new interviewees in the office every other day. I’m excited to follow the progress of DocASAP in years to come.

Most importantly, the people that I’ve met and worked with here at DocASAP have made my time as an intern comfortable, rewarding, and fun. The term “strong family spirit” is aptly used on the DocASAP Team Page. The small team (~20 people here at the headquarters) and the colorful, bright, open office, replete with beanbag chairs and bouncy balls and void of cubicles (see above picture), contribute to making DocASAP a welcoming, unified work environment. Perhaps most important to me, a broke college student spending summer away from home for the first time, were the catered lunches. Every day, the company’s employees congregated in the psuedo-kitchen to eat together. Free food is great, but breaking bread allowed me to get to know a group of kind, intelligent, and humorous people I’m not soon to forget.
